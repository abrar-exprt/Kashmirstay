Must-See

Top Attractions in Srinagar

From ancient Mughal gardens to the floating world of Dal Lake — Srinagar rewards every kind of traveller.

Iconic · 18 km²
Dal Lake & Houseboats
The jewel of Srinagar — heritage wooden houseboats, shikara rides through floating gardens, the famous Dal floating market. Stay on the water for the most unforgettable Kashmir night.
Mughal Garden · 1619 AD
Shalimar Bagh
Built by Emperor Jahangir for Empress Nur Jahan — four terraced gardens descending to Dal Lake with chinar-shaded walkways and ornate fountains. Open all year, most beautiful in spring.
Mughal Garden · 1633 AD
Nishat Bagh
The "Garden of Bliss" — the largest Mughal garden in the Indian subcontinent with 12 terraced levels overlooking Dal Lake and the Pir Panjal mountains. A UNESCO heritage nominee.
Temple · 2,000+ years old
Shankaracharya Temple
Perched atop a 1,100ft hill above Dal Lake, this ancient Shiva temple offers the most panoramic view of Srinagar. Best visited at sunrise or sunset. 243 steps to the top.
Sacred Shrine · Dal Lake Shore
Hazratbal Shrine
The holiest Muslim shrine in Kashmir — a stunning white marble mosque on the shores of Dal Lake, holding a relic of Prophet Muhammad. Serene, architecturally breathtaking.
Asia's Largest · March–April
Indira Gandhi Tulip Garden
Over 1.5 million tulips in 64 varieties carpet this terraced hillside facing Dal Lake every spring. Asia's largest tulip garden — a photographer's dream that draws visitors from 50+ countries.

Arrival & Dal Lake Magic

Travel Smart

Srinagar Travel Tips

💡
Book Houseboats in Advance
Peak season (Apr–Jun, Oct) sees houseboats booked out weeks ahead. Always book through verified MBP agencies for guaranteed quality and safety.
🌤️
Pack Layers in Every Season
Even summer nights on Dal Lake can be cool at 1,585m. Always carry a light jacket. Winters are cold (-2 to 10°C) but magical.
🚖
Use Pre-Paid Taxis from Airport
Srinagar Airport has official pre-paid taxi booths. Agree on fares in advance. Most verified agencies include airport transfers.
💰
Carry Cash for Local Markets
Most Dal Lake vendors, shikara owners and bazaar shops prefer cash. ATMs are available at Lal Chowk and major hotels. UPI works widely.
🛍️
Buy Handicrafts at Emporiums
For authentic Kashmiri pashmina, carpets and papier-mâché, visit J&K govt emporiums at Polo View or Lal Chowk to avoid tourist-trap pricing.
📱
SIM Cards for Tourists
Airtel and Jio work well in Srinagar. Local SIM cards available at the airport with valid ID. BSNL has better coverage in remote areas.
Your Questions

Srinagar FAQ

What is the best time to visit Srinagar? +
March to June is the most popular season — tulips bloom, weather is pleasant (10–25°C) and Dal Lake is at its most beautiful. October–November brings stunning chinar autumn colours. December–February is great for those who love a cold, romantic winter escape. July–August monsoon can be humid with some road disruptions.
How much does a Dal Lake houseboat cost? +
Houseboat prices range from ₹1,500/night (B-class budget) to ₹20,000/night (A-class luxury heritage). Most mid-range options fall between ₹4,000–₹8,000/night for a couple, including breakfast, dinner and a shikara ride. Always book through MBP-verified agencies to ensure quality and safety certification.
How do I get to Srinagar from Delhi? +
Srinagar Airport (SXR) has direct flights from Delhi (1.5 hrs), Mumbai (2.5 hrs), Bangalore (3 hrs), Hyderabad and Dubai. One-way flights from Delhi start around ₹3,000–₹8,000. The road journey from Delhi via Jammu is 18–20 hours. No direct trains — trains go to Banihal (3 hrs from Srinagar).
Is Srinagar safe for foreign tourists? +
Yes. Srinagar's tourist areas — Dal Lake, Mughal gardens, Lal Chowk — are safe and well-patrolled. Srinagar welcomed record tourists in 2024–2025, including thousands from USA, UK, UAE, Canada, Australia and Israel. Travel with a verified agency for local guidance and peace of mind.
What is the best Srinagar itinerary for 3 days? +
Day 1: Dal Lake houseboat, shikara ride, floating market. Day 2: Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh, Shankaracharya Temple, Lal Chowk. Day 3: Hazratbal shrine, old city, handicraft shopping, Nagin Lake. Use MBP AI planner for a personalised day-by-day guide.
