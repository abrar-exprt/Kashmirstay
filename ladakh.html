Acclimatization is Essential in Leh
Leh city sits at 3,524m. All visitors — regardless of fitness — must rest for 24–48 hours on arrival before any activity. Avoid alcohol and heavy meals. Stay hydrated. Diamox (altitude sickness medication) is available at Leh pharmacies. Your agency will advise on acclimatization protocol.
Must-See

Top Attractions in Leh Ladakh

Every turn in Ladakh is a photograph — from turquoise high-altitude lakes to ancient cliff monasteries.

Iconic Lake · 4,350m
Pangong Tso Lake
The world-famous blue lake at 4,350m — 134 km long, shifting from turquoise to blue to violet as clouds cross. Made globally iconic by the film 3 Idiots. Overnight camping on the shore is unmissable.
Desert Valley · 3,048m
Nubra Valley
Sand dunes in a high-altitude Himalayan desert — where Bactrian double-humped camels still walk the ancient Silk Route. The Diskit Monastery's giant Buddha statue overlooks the valley. Otherworldly.
Mountain Pass · 5,359m
Khardung La Pass
One of the world's highest motorable passes — the legendary gateway to Nubra Valley. The road here at 5,359m is a rite of passage for every Ladakh biker and road tripper. Snow and thin air year-round.
Buddhist Monastery · 1630 AD
Hemis Monastery
Ladakh's largest and wealthiest monastery — built in 1630, home of the annual Hemis Festival (June/July) with masked dance performances. A 45-km drive from Leh through a dramatic gorge.
Cliff Monastery · 12 Storeys
Thiksey Monastery
The most photographed monastery in Ladakh — 12 storeys of white buildings cascading down a cliff face like a mini Potala Palace. Houses a 15m-tall Maitreya Buddha statue. Dawn prayers begin at 6am.
Natural Phenomenon
Magnetic Hill & Gurudwara Pathar Sahib
The famous optical illusion where vehicles appear to roll uphill. Adjacent Gurudwara Pathar Sahib is a sacred Sikh shrine set in dramatic Ladakhi landscape — a spiritual and scientific curiosity in one stop.

Arrival & Acclimatization in Leh
Arrive at Leh Airport (3,524m). Rest is mandatory — do not plan any activity on Day 1. Light walk around Leh market in the evening if feeling well. Drink plenty of water. No alcohol. Your body needs time to adjust.

Your Questions

Leh Ladakh FAQ

What is the best time to visit Leh Ladakh? +
June to September is peak season — all passes open, Pangong and Nubra accessible, days 10–25°C. July–August is peak with the Hemis Festival. May and October offer fewer crowds. Winter (Nov–Feb) is for the Chadar Trek on the frozen Zanskar River — cold (-20°C) but magical.
How do I get to Leh Ladakh? +
By air: Daily flights from Delhi (1 hr, ₹4,000–₹12,000), Mumbai (2.5 hrs), Srinagar (40 min) to Leh Airport (IXL). By road: Srinagar–Leh via Zoji La (434 km, 2 days), or Manali–Leh (479 km, 2 days via 5 high passes) — both open June–October. The Manali–Leh highway is one of the world's greatest road trips.
Do I need a permit for Leh Ladakh? +
Indian nationals don't need a permit for Leh city. Inner Line Permits (ILP) are required for Pangong Tso, Nubra Valley, Tso Moriri and border areas — ₹400–₹600/person, easily arranged at the DC office in Leh or through your agency. Foreign nationals need Protected Area Permits (PAP) for certain zones.
How many days is ideal for Leh Ladakh? +
Minimum 7 days: 2 days Leh & monasteries, 2 days Pangong Lake, 2 days Nubra Valley, 1 day acclimatization. 10–12 days lets you add Zanskar Valley, Tso Moriri and the Manali road. Do not rush Ladakh — altitude sickness is real and acclimatization is non-negotiable.
What is altitude sickness in Leh and how to prevent it? +
Altitude sickness (AMS) occurs because Leh sits at 3,524m with 30% less oxygen than sea level. Symptoms: headache, nausea, dizziness. Prevention: rest 24–48 hours on arrival, drink 3+ litres of water daily, avoid alcohol, walk slowly, take Diamox if advised. Flying directly from sea level is fine — just rest on arrival.
