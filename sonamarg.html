Top Attractions in Sonamarg

From glacier snowfields to mountain passes — Sonamarg packs extraordinary adventure into every kilometre.

Glacier · Snow Year-Round
Thajiwas Glacier
A massive glacier just 3 km from town — a stunning ice river descending from 4,000m peaks. Pony ride or 4 km walk. Snow activities even in July. The valley's most iconic sight and must-do.
Adventure · 3,500m
Zero Point
At 3,500m near the Zoji La pass — permanent snow, spectacular mountain panoramas and the thrill of standing at India's frontier. Accessible by SUV, only May–October. Most dramatic viewpoint in the region.
Mountain Pass · 3,528m
Zoji La Pass
The dramatic gateway between Kashmir and Ladakh at 3,528m — where the road clings to sheer cliff faces above glacial valleys. One of the world's most thrilling high-altitude drives on NH1.
River · Trout Fishing
Sindh River
The Sindh River through Sonamarg is Kashmir's premier trout-fishing destination — brown trout and rainbow trout in crystal-clear Himalayan water. Fishing permits from J&K fisheries department.
Camping · Amarnath Base
Baltal Camp
The base camp for the short Amarnath Yatra route — a beautiful riverside camping spot with dramatic glacier views. Also accessible by helicopter for pilgrims and adventure travellers.
Trek · High Altitude
Gangabal Lake Trek
A 5-day highland trek from Naranag to Sonamarg passing twin alpine lakes Vishansar and Krishansar at 4,000m+ — one of India's most beautiful and unspoiled Himalayan treks.

Sonamarg FAQ

How far is Sonamarg from Srinagar? +
Sonamarg is 87 km from Srinagar — approximately 2.5–3 hours by road on NH1 through the scenic Sindh Valley. Private taxis cost ₹2,500–₹3,000 one-way. Sonamarg is open May–October; the road closes November–April due to heavy snowfall on Zoji La.
What is Zero Point in Sonamarg? +
Zero Point is a high-altitude (3,500m) snow-covered location near the Zoji La pass, approximately 15 km from Sonamarg town. It's accessible by SUV (₹3,000–₹4,000/vehicle). Even in July–August, Zero Point has 4–6 feet of permanent snow — perfect for snowball fights and alpine photography.
Can I camp in Sonamarg? +
Yes — camping is Sonamarg's signature experience. Luxury tent camps with proper bedding, heating (kangri fire pots) and Kashmiri meals are available from ₹2,500/night/tent. The campsite near Thajiwas Glacier is most popular. Always book through verified MBP agencies for safety and quality.
Is Sonamarg open in winter? +
No. Sonamarg closes November–April due to 10–15 feet of snowfall blocking the Zoji La pass. The season is May–October. In some years the road opens late April. Gulmarg is the alternative for winter/snow experiences. Plan Sonamarg only for the May–October window.
Can I go from Sonamarg to Leh Ladakh directly? +
Yes — Sonamarg is the last major stop in Kashmir before Zoji La pass and Ladakh. The drive from Sonamarg to Leh takes 7–9 hours (226 km) via Kargil. This is the NH1 route — one of the world's greatest mountain road trips. Best done May–October, ideally with a verified tour operator who knows the road.
