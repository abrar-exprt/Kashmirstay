Inner Line Permit (ILP) Required for Gurez Valley
Gurez Valley is a restricted frontier zone — all visitors (Indian and foreign nationals) need an Inner Line Permit before entry. ILPs are issued at the Bandipora DC office or through verified agencies in Srinagar. The permit process is simple and usually completed same-day. Your KashmirStay agency will arrange this for you.
The Legend

Why Gurez is Kashmir's Secret

Gurez Valley sits just 6 km from the Line of Control — a frontier valley that remained closed to outsiders for decades. When it finally opened to tourists, it revealed something rare: a Kashmir completely untouched by commercialisation.

The Dard tribe — descended from Alexander the Great's soldiers according to local legend — still live here in wooden houses with intricate carvings, speaking Shina, one of India's most ancient languages.

The Habba Khatoon peak (4,742m) is named after Kashmir's most beloved poetess — a 16th-century shepherd girl turned queen whose love poetry is still recited in Kashmir today. It watches over the valley like a guardian.

Must-Experience

What to See in Gurez Valley

Gurez rewards the traveller who seeks genuine experiences — not tourist infrastructure, but living culture and raw natural beauty.

River · All Valley
Kishanganga River
The turquoise-green Kishanganga (Neelum River in Pakistan) flows through the entire valley — crystal-clear Himalayan water perfect for trout fishing, riverside walks and reflection photography at dawn.
Peak · 4,742m
Habba Khatoon Peak
The iconic snow-covered pyramid peak named after Kashmir's beloved poetess Zoon Razdan (Habba Khatoon). The peak is visible from Dawar village and dominates the valley skyline — a truly haunting silhouette.
Village · Cultural Heart
Dawar Village
The main village and administrative centre of Gurez — wooden houses with ornate carved balconies, the only basic guesthouses and the starting point for valley treks. Visit the local market on Fridays.
Sub-Valley · Remote
Tulail Valley
Beyond Gurez, Tulail is even more pristine — an untouched sub-valley with alpine meadows, nomadic shepherd camps and views of the 5,000m+ Himalayan range. Barely 200 tourists visit each year.
Ancient Ruins · Historical
Sharda Temple Ruins
Near the Line of Control — ruins of the ancient Sharda Peeth, one of the 18 shakti peethas of Hinduism and a great university centre from 800–1200 AD. A place of profound historical significance now being restored.
Stargazing · No Light Pollution
Dark Sky Stargazing
Zero light pollution and high altitude make Gurez one of the best dark sky locations in all of India. The Milky Way is visible on clear nights — camping here and gazing at the star canopy is an experience like no other.

Your Questions

Gurez Valley FAQ

Do I need a permit to visit Gurez Valley? +
Yes. Gurez Valley is a restricted Inner Line area bordering Pakistan. All visitors — Indian and foreign nationals — require an Inner Line Permit (ILP). The permit is issued at the Bandipora DC office or arranged by your Srinagar agency (takes 1 day). MBP-verified agencies handle all permit paperwork for you.
How far is Gurez Valley from Srinagar? +
Gurez Valley is 123 km from Srinagar — 4–5 hours by road, crossing the Razdan Pass (3,300m). The road is well-maintained but winding. Depart Srinagar by 7–8am to arrive comfortably. The drive through the Bandipora mountains is itself spectacular.
What is the best time to visit Gurez Valley? +
June–September is the best season — wildflowers bloom, the Kishanganga is full, Tulail meadows are green and nights are clear for stargazing. The valley is closed November–April due to heavy snowfall and road closure at Razdan Pass. October can be cold but offers stunning autumn colours.
Is there mobile network or ATM in Gurez Valley? +
Mobile network is very limited in Gurez — BSNL has patchy coverage in Dawar. No Jio or Airtel signal in most of the valley. There are no ATMs in Gurez — bring sufficient cash from Srinagar. The disconnect is part of Gurez's magic, but plan accordingly. Your agency will advise on communication.
